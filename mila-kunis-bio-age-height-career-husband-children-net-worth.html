Biography of Mila Kunis

Milena Markovna “Mila” Kunis, born on August 14, 1983, is a Ukrainian-American actress known for her versatile career in film and television. She was born in Chernivtsi, Ukraine, and later moved to Los Angeles, California, where her journey in entertainment began.

At the age of 14, Kunis made her mark as Jackie Burkhart in the popular Fox television series “That ’70s Show,” a role she portrayed from 1998 to 2006. Her talent quickly caught the industry’s attention, and she embarked on a successful voice acting career, voicing Meg Griffin in the Fox animated series “Family Guy” from 1999 onwards.

Kunis’s breakout in the film industry came in 2008 when she starred in the romantic comedy “Forgetting Sarah Marshall.” Her performance earned critical acclaim and set the stage for her future success. In 2010, she delivered an exceptional performance in the psychological thriller “Black Swan,” for which she received the Marcello Mastroianni Award for Best Young Actor or Actress and earned nominations for the SAG Award and the Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress.
