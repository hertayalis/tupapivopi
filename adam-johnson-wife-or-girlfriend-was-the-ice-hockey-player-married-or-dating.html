Who was Adam Johnson?

Adam Johnson was an American professional ice hockey forward. He played 13 games in the National Hockey League with the Pittsburgh Penguins during the 2018–19 and 2019–20 seasons.

Adam Johnson biography

Johnson, a native of Hibbing, Minnesota, began his hockey journey after completing his high school education. He honed his skills in the United States Hockey League (USHL) by playing for the Indiana Ice and Sioux City Musketeers.

Despite going undrafted, Johnson showcased his talent during his time at the University of Minnesota Duluth, where he competed in the National Collegiate Hockey Conference (NCHC) for two seasons. During his sophomore year with the Bulldogs in the 2016-2017 season, Johnson’s exceptional performance stood out.

He impressively scored 18 goals and accumulated 37 points in 42 games, securing the second-highest goal and point totals on the team.

Notably, Johnson’s pivotal moment came when he netted an overtime, power-play goal to secure Minnesota-Duluth’s spot in the 2017 Frozen Four, defeating Boston University with a score of 3-2. However, despite their efforts, Johnson and the Bulldogs ultimately fell short in the NCAA championship game, losing 3-2 to the University of Denver.

Adam Johnson career

Johnson chose to sign a contract with the Malmö Redhawks of the Swedish Hockey League (SHL) on December 15, 2020, for the 2020–21 season due to the COVID-19 pandemic causing delays in the North American season.

In his role as a top-six forward, he contributed seven goals and 12 points in 21 regular-season games before deciding to terminate his contract and return to North America on March 27, 2020. On April 6, 2021, Johnson became a free agent and signed with the Ontario Reign of the AHL, which serves as the primary affiliate of the Los Angeles Kings, for the remainder of the 2020–21 season.

Making a significant impact on the offensive end, he recorded 11 points in just 14 games. Continuing with the Reign for the subsequent 2021–22 season, Johnson accumulated one goal and six points in 28 regular-season games before being traded to the Lehigh Valley Phantoms in exchange for future considerations on February 17, 2022.

Adam Johnson cause of death: How did die?

Johnson, a player for the Nottingham Panthers, tragically passed away following an unforeseen incident during a game in England on Saturday.

The unfortunate event occurred during a Challenge Cup game against the Sheffield Steelers at Sheffield’s Utilita Arena, where Johnson sustained a severe neck injury in the second period. Despite being promptly transported to Northern General Hospital, the dedicated efforts of the physicians were regrettably unable to preserve his life.

“The Nottingham Panthers are truly devastated to announce that Adam Johnson has tragically passed away following a freak accident at the game in Sheffield last night,” the team said Sunday morning.

“The Panthers would like to send our thoughts and condolences to Adam’s family, his partner, and all his friends at this extremely difficult time. Everyone at the club including players, staff, management and ownership are heartbroken at the news of Adam’s passing.”
