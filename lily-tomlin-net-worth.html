Lifestyle

Talented actress and comedian Lily Tomlin lives a luxurious life with a net worth of $20 million.

She was born in Detroit, Michigan, in 1939 and grew up with a younger brother Richard.

Both of her parents were Southern Baptists from Paducah, Kentucky.

She attended Wayne State University after finishing Cass Technical High School, where she studied biology before changing her focus to the theatre.

After graduating, Tomlin began performing stand-up comedy in Detroit and New York City.

Similarly, Lily went on to study acting at the HB Studio in New York City’s Greenwich Village.

She got her first television appearance on “The Merv Griffin Show” in 1965.

The following year, she was cast in the final season of “The Garry Moore Show.”

Lily met her future spouse, writer Jane Wagner, in 1971. In New York, they were introduced through a mutual friend, actress Betty Beaird.

Lily Tomlin | Charity

Tomlin is well-known for supporting charitable groups, notably those concerned with animal welfare, women’s rights, and the LGBTQ community.

She has performed for numerous organizations in the USA, including The Ann Richards School and other community action groups.

Lily has also helped improve lives by establishing the “Lily Tomlin/Jane Wagner Cultural Arts Center” at the Los Angeles LGBT Center.

She and her wife co-founded and have been active in the growth of the Goosebump Garden at Boston’s internationally renowned “LGBT Fenway Health Center” for over 25 years.

Similarly, she appeared alongside comedian Kathy Griffin at a 2019 block party to commemorate the opening of the Los Angeles LGBT Center’s new campus.

The Anita May Rosenstein Campus opened over two years ago and is billed as the world’s first intergenerational LGBTQ institution.

There are drop-in facilities for youth and elders on the Campus, as well as 100 rooms for homeless youth.

Furthermore, Tomlin advocates for the relocation of elephants from zoos to sanctuaries where they can enjoy their lives in a better and happier environment.

Lily Tomlin | Career

After temporarily hosting the ABC show “Music Scene” in 1969, Tomlin’s career took off when she got the role in the NBC sketch comedy “Rowan & Martin’s Laugh-In.”

She was an instant hit on the show, receiving attention for the many new characters she invented. Her most famous roles were Ernestine, Edith Ann, Mrs. Earbore, and Lucille the Rubber Freak.

Lily also appeared in male drag as Tommy Velour and Rick, earning her one of the first notable female comedians to do so.

After “Laugh-In,” Tomlin co-wrote and acted in “Lily,” a CBS variety show special.

Throughout the rest of the 1970s and 1980s, she frequently appeared as herself on numerous television shows.

She also won Emmys for three television specials, “The Lily Tomlin Special” in 1975, “The Paul Simon Special” in 1977, and “Lily: Sold Out” in 1981.

Following an almost ten-year hiatus from television, Lily made her comeback in the television film “And the Band Played On.”
