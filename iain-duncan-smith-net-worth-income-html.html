George Iain Duncan Smith (IDS) is a British Conservative Party politician and the current Secretary of State for Work and Pensions. He was born on April 9, 195From 2001 to 2003, he was the Conservative Party’s Leader and Leader of the Opposition. He was initially elected to Parliament as the Member of Parliament for Chingford in 1992, and since 1997, he has represented the successor seat of Chingford and Woodford Green. Duncan Smith was born in Edinburgh and served in the Scots Guards from 1975 to 1981, with deployments in Northern Ireland and Rhodesia under his belt.

 In 1981, he joined the Conservative Party and was elected to the House of Commons in 199Duncan Smith took over as Conservative Leader from William Hague in 2001, winning the election largely because to Margaret Thatcher’s backing for his Eurosceptic philosophy. Duncan Smith was the first Roman Catholic to lead the Conservative Party, as well as the first since Arthur Balfour to be born in Scotland. He was also the first leader of any British political party from an ethnic minority background, since he is of Japanese ancestry.

 He was designated one of Britain’s most prominent Roman Catholics by “The Tablet” in 20During his tenure as Conservative Leader, his party’s poll numbers plummeted, and many Conservative MPs began to believe he was incapable of winning an election. His MPs voted no confidence in his leadership in 2003, prompting him to quit and be replaced by Michael Howard. He established the centre-right Centre for Social Justice, an independent think tank from the Conservative Party, and became a published writer as a backbencher, but his book The Devil’s Tune garnered harsh criticism. Duncan Smith was appointed Secretary of State for Work and Pensions by Prime Minister David Cameron on May 12, 2010.
